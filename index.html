Interacting With Data: Voter Trends and Turnouts

Part 1 - Voting habits

Does frequency of voting change with income and education levels?

This parallel categories plot aims to display the relationship between voting frequency, education, and income. High voting frequency (always vote) is shown to be correlated with higher education levels as well as a higher education. The opposite trend is also displayed. Those who rarely or never vote tend to have some college or less.



Part 2 - Voter turnout and absentee voting through the years

How did voter turnout and absentee voting change in Virginia General Elections from 2014 to 2022?

The bubble plot aims to visualize and emphasize the differences in percent turnout and percent absentee voting between 2014-2022 with a simple animation. Here we can see that 2020 looks much different from the rest of the years. This year has a much higher percent of absentee voters than all other years, as shown by the bubbles being much higher on the y-axis. This is very likely, if not definitely, due to the effects of the Covid-19 pandemic preventing large gatherings, thus absentee ballots were encouraged to stop the spread of Covid-19



Part 3 - Map of voter turnout in Virginia counties and cities

What did voter turnout look like in cities and counties in Virginia in the 2022 general election?

Here, the percent turnout for different cities and counties in Virginia is displayed on a choropleth map to allow for easier visualization of regional differences. The darker shades of green tend to be concentrated in the central/upper regions of Virginia. The western regions of Virginia also tend to have much lower turnouts than anywhere else. The map also displays a positive gradient in voter turnout when travelling from western to eastern Virginia.
